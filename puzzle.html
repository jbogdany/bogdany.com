puzzle — rules

  • Every piece is cut fresh, with a randomized interlocking shape — no two puzzles have the same tab-and-blank pattern, even on the same picture.
  • Drag a piece with your mouse or finger and drop it near its correct spot — the dashed rectangle marks where the finished picture belongs. Get close enough and it snaps into place and locks.
  • The faint image inside the dashed rectangle is a reference — toggle it off with [preview] for a harder, more traditional puzzle.
  • Type a number of pieces (up to 500) into the pieces box and click [apply] — the grid is rounded up to the nearest shape that fits your target and the image's proportions, so the actual count may land slightly above what you typed.
  • [shuffle] re-cuts the same picture into a brand new set of shapes and re-scatters them. [new image] fetches a fresh random photo.
  • You can also paste any image URL into the box and click [load] to build a puzzle from your own picture.
