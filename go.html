go — rules

  • Players alternate placing stones on the intersections of the grid, not the squares.
  • A stone or connected group is captured and removed the moment it has no remaining liberties — no adjacent empty points.
  • You can't play a stone that leaves your own group with zero liberties, unless doing so captures enemy stones first (suicide is illegal).
  • Ko rule: you can't immediately replay a move that would recreate the exact position your opponent just captured into.
  • Passing twice in a row ends the game.
  • Scoring is by area: your stones plus the empty territory they surround, with a small komi bonus added for white to offset black's first-move advantage. Higher total wins.
