Suzen — Rules

Object

Create a passage through your opponent's defenses and cross into their home territory (their back row) to win. A piece that reaches the opponent's home territory cannot be captured.

Setup

Each side has twelve pieces: six "1" pieces on the front line, six "2" pieces on the back-row home territory.

Movement

Pieces move orthogonally only — forward, backward, left, or right, never diagonally, and only in one direction per turn. Each piece's four small numbers show the maximum squares it may travel in each direction, relative to the way it's facing:

  • No. 1 piece: 1 forward, 2 right, 3 backward, 4 left.
  • No. 2 piece: 2 forward, 3 right, 4 backward, 1 left.

A piece always moves the exact number of squares shown for that direction — never fewer, never more — and only if that exact square stays on the board.

Jumping & capturing

Pieces may jump over any piece — their own or an opponent's — along their path. A square may never be landed on if it holds one of your own pieces. Any opponent piece that is jumped over, or landed on, is captured and removed from the board. Up to four opponent pieces can be captured in a single move.

Winning

The moment a piece crosses into the opponent's home territory, the game ends immediately. The game can also be won by capturing every one of the opponent's pieces, though this is rare. The game cannot end in a tie.

Computer opponent

Easy picks a random legal move. Medium favors captures and forward progress, with a little randomness. Hard looks a full round ahead — your best reply included — and avoids moves that hand you an easy win.

Tips

  • Defend "1" pieces with "2" pieces.
  • Leaving too many "2" pieces at home is a weak strategy.
  • Neither piece type is stronger — they're strong in different ways, and you'll need both for a good endgame.
