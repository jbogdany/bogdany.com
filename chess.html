chess — rules

  • Goal: checkmate the opponent's king — trap it so no move escapes capture.
  • Pawns move forward one square (two on their first move) and capture diagonally. A pawn reaching the far rank promotes — here it always becomes a queen. En passant is supported.
  • Knights jump in an L-shape and can hop over other pieces.
  • Bishops slide diagonally, rooks slide in straight lines, and the queen combines both.
  • The king moves one square in any direction. It may castle with a rook if neither has moved and the squares between them are empty and unattacked.
  • Check means the king is under attack and must be addressed immediately. Checkmate ends the game. Stalemate — no legal moves, but not in check — is a draw.
