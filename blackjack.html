blackjack — rules

  • Bet chips, then deal. You and the dealer each get two cards; one of the dealer's cards stays face down.
  • Cards 2–10 are worth face value, face cards are worth 10, and an Ace is worth 11 or 1 — whichever keeps your hand from busting.
  • On your turn: Hit to take another card, Stand to end your turn, or Double Down (first two cards only) to double your bet, take exactly one more card, and stand automatically.
  • Going over 21 is a bust — you lose your bet immediately, before the dealer even plays.
  • A natural blackjack (21 on the first two cards) pays 3:2, unless the dealer also has one, which pushes.
  • Once you stand, the dealer reveals the hidden card and must keep hitting until reaching 17 or more.
  • If the dealer busts, or your total beats theirs, you win even money on your bet. Equal totals push — your bet is returned.
  • A fresh shuffled shoe is used for every single hand.
