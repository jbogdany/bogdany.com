Yahtzee — Rules

  • Each turn you roll five dice, then may re-roll any of them up to two more times (three rolls total), setting aside — "holding" — the dice you want to keep between rolls.
  • After your final roll, place the result into one open category on your scorecard. Every category can only be used once per game, and if nothing fits you can still lock in a category for zero.
  • Upper section (ones through sixes): score the sum of just that number's dice. Filling all six with a combined total of 63 or more earns a 35-point bonus.
  • Three/Four of a Kind: score the sum of all five dice if at least three (or four) show the same value.
  • Full House: three of one value and two of another — 25 points.
  • Small Straight (four in a row): 30 points. Large Straight (five in a row): 40 points.
  • Yahtzee (all five dice matching): 50 points. Rolling a second one after your Yahtzee box is already filled earns a 100-point bonus, and that roll can still be placed in any open category.
  • Chance: score the sum of all five dice, no pattern required — a safe fallback for an unlucky roll.
  • The game ends when both scorecards are full. Highest grand total wins.
